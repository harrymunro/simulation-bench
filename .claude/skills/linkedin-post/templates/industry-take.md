# Template: Industry Take / Hot Take Post

## Best rhetoric devices
- **Antithesis** for contrasting positions
- **Anaphora** for building your argument (but break the pattern, see anti-patterns)
- **Hyperbaton** for attention-grabbing openings

## Approach. NOT a fill-in-the-blanks template

Do NOT follow this as a rigid structure. The provocative/industry-take post is the MOST at risk of AI detection because "claim, evidence, conclusion" is exactly the structure language models default to. You need to be deliberately messier.

### Shape 1: Start with the conclusion
Lead with your verdict. "Commercial simulation software is a con." Then backfill the reasoning. This feels more like someone who actually holds the opinion, they lead with it because they believe it, not because they built an argument to get there.

### Shape 2: Bury the evidence in a story
Don't lay out your argument like a debate. Tell a story about something that happened, a project, a conversation, an invoice, and let the opinion emerge from the specifics. The reader should arrive at your conclusion before you state it.

### Shape 3: The uneven rant
Let it build unevenly. Some points get a paragraph, some get half a sentence. Let one tangent go slightly too long. Circle back. This reads like someone actually thinking through their frustration, not composing an op-ed.

### Shape 4: The short, blunt take
Sometimes the hot take is three lines. The opinion, one piece of evidence, full stop. Not everything provocative needs to be argued at length. Confidence in brevity is inherently human.

## Example

```
I stopped paying for simulation software last year. Saved
about £8,000 and genuinely cannot identify what I lost.

The models are the same. Python and SimPy, same logic I
was implementing in Arena anyway, just without the GUI
that I never used and the licence manager that crashed
every other Tuesday.

People keep asking me "but what about enterprise features?"
and I keep asking them to name one. Specifically. They
usually can't, or they name something SimPy does natively,
or they name something nobody on their team has ever
actually used.

I'm not saying commercial tools are worthless. I'm saying
their pricing assumes you have no alternatives, and now
you do. The sales team just isn't going to be the one to
tell you that.

#Simulation #OpenSource #Python #Engineering
```

## Anti-patterns to avoid

These are the AI tells most common in provocative/industry posts:

- **The triple anaphora**: "You don't need X. You don't need Y. You don't need Z." Three parallel lines building to a crescendo is AI's favourite rhetorical move. If you're repeating a structure, break it on the second or third iteration. Vary the sentence lengths. Change one to a different construction entirely.
- **"The uncomfortable truth:"**: this phrase specifically is now an AI fingerprint. Just state the truth. If it's uncomfortable, the reader will feel that without you labelling it.
- **Claim, evidence, conclusion structure**: this is essay structure and it's exactly what AI produces. Real opinions don't build cleanly. They start in the middle, loop back, include tangents about that one time you wasted a month on something, and land unevenly.
- **The balanced caveat**: "I'm not saying X is bad, I'm saying Y is better" is fine once. But if every bold claim gets immediately softened with a qualifier, you're writing like a language model that's been trained to be even-handed. Commit to your take.
- **Closing with "Worth considering" or "Food for thought"**: soft, hedge-y endings that sound diplomatic rather than opinionated. Either end with conviction or just stop. Don't trail off politely.

## Voice notes for hot takes

- Take a clear stance, fence-sitting is boring and reads as AI
- Back opinions with experience, not just opinion. A specific story beats an abstract argument.
- "I could be wrong, but..." is okay occasionally, genuine uncertainty reads as human
- Invite disagreement, engagement is the goal
- Don't punch down, criticise systems, not people
- Your frustration should feel specific and personal, not like a think-piece
