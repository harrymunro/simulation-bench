# Content Pillars

Three pillars that balance lead generation with thought leadership.

---

## 1. Technical (40% of content)

**Purpose**: Demonstrate expertise, provide value, attract practitioners

**Goal alignment**: Lead generation (shows you know your stuff)

### Topics
- SimPy tutorials and code walkthroughs
- Python tips for simulation modellers
- Discrete-event simulation concepts explained
- Performance optimisation techniques
- Data visualisation for simulation results
- Integration with pandas, matplotlib, etc.
- Debugging simulation models
- Testing strategies for simulations

### Formats
- "Here's how I do X" with code snippets
- Before/after comparisons
- Common mistakes and how to fix them
- Quick tips (single concept posts)
- Deep dives (for newsletters)

### Voice notes
- Teaching tone, slightly less provocative
- Show real code, real numbers
- "Here's what actually works..."
- Share shortcuts you've learned

---

## 2. Industry Commentary (35% of content)

**Purpose**: Challenge status quo, build thought leadership, differentiate

**Goal alignment**: Thought leadership (positions you as the voice of change)

### Topics
- Why commercial simulation software is overpriced
- Vendor lock-in and its hidden costs
- Open source vs proprietary debate
- The future of simulation (AI integration, cloud, etc.)
- Industry trends and what they mean
- Critiques of common practices
- Python's growing role in engineering
- What simulation consultancies get wrong

### Formats
- Hot takes (strong opinions, clearly stated)
- "The uncomfortable truth about..."
- Myth-busting posts
- Trend analysis
- Contrarian views

### Voice notes
- Maximum provocation lives here
- Take a clear stance - no fence-sitting
- Back opinions with experience
- "Nobody wants to say this, but..."

---

## 3. Personal & Career Stories (25% of content)

**Purpose**: Build connection, show authenticity, inspire

**Goal alignment**: Both (trust leads to conversions; stories get shared)

### Topics
- Your journey from engineer to educator
- Mistakes you've made and lessons learned
- Pivotal career moments
- Behind the scenes of building the business
- What you're currently working on/struggling with
- Book recommendations and what you're learning
- Engineering culture and mindset
- Work-life balance as an engineer-entrepreneur

### Formats
- "Three years ago, I..." narratives
- Lessons from failure
- Day-in-the-life glimpses
- Reflections and realisations
- Milestone celebrations (authentic ones)

### Voice notes
- Most vulnerable and personal
- Okay to not have all the answers
- Stories where you're not the hero
- End with actionable takeaway

---

## Content Mix Guidelines

### Weekly LinkedIn rhythm (3-4 posts)
- 1-2 Technical posts
- 1 Industry commentary
- 1 Personal/career story

### Monthly email rhythm (4-5 emails)
- 2 Nurture emails (mix of all pillars)
- 1 Technical deep-dive
- 1 Industry perspective
- 1 Launch/promotional (when relevant)

### Balancing goals

| Content Type | Lead Gen Focus | Thought Leadership Focus |
|--------------|---------------|-------------------------|
| Technical tutorial | High | Medium |
| Industry hot take | Low | Very High |
| Personal story | Medium | High |
| Launch email | Very High | Low |
| Newsletter | Medium | Medium |

---

## Seasonal Considerations

### High-activity periods
- New Year (fresh starts, learning goals)
- September (back to learning after summer)
- Budget planning season (October-November)

### Launch windows
- Align course launches with high-activity periods
- Build up with industry commentary
- Follow with technical proof posts

### Evergreen content
- Technical tutorials work year-round
- Personal stories can be scheduled anytime
- Industry takes work best when timely
